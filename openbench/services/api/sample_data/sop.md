# Sample Prep SOP — v1.3

**Document type:** Standard Operating Procedure
**Process:** Aliquoting an aqueous reference standard for downstream HPLC analysis.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-10  **Owner:** QC Lab Supervisor

## 1. Purpose

This procedure describes the steps to safely aliquot a non-hazardous aqueous reference standard
("Reagent A — 1 g/L") into labeled sample tubes and prepare the bench for the next operator.

## 2. Scope

QC operators trained on the laboratory's general chemical hygiene plan (CHP). This SOP does
not authorize substitutions of reagents, containers, or temperatures.

## 3. Personnel and authorization

- Operator must be CHP-trained.
- New staff must complete a supervised run before independent use.

## 4. Materials

- Reagent A (Reference standard, 1 g/L aqueous solution) — supplied in a clear screw-cap
  amber bottle labeled with lot number and expiration date.
- 8 × 2 mL polypropylene sample tubes with secure caps.
- 1 × secondary containment tray (white plastic, ~30 × 20 cm).
- 1 × disposable polypropylene pipette tip (1 mL volume).
- 1 × calibrated 1000 µL pipette (model BP-1000; see equipment manual).
- 1 × waste container labeled "Aqueous waste — Reagent A".

## 5. Required PPE

- Lab coat (buttoned)
- ANSI Z87.1 safety glasses or splash goggles
- Nitrile gloves (compatible with Reagent A — see SDS section 8)

## 6. Engineering controls

- Bench surface in a well-ventilated area.
- Spill kit within arm's reach (operator confirms before starting).

## 7. Procedure

### Step 1 — Confirm documents

Verify you have the **current** SOP, the SDS for Reagent A, and the BP-1000 pipette manual.
If any document is missing, **stop** and consult your supervisor.

### Step 2 — Confirm PPE

Don the required PPE listed in section 5. Inspect gloves for tears.

### Step 3 — Stage materials

Place the 8 sample tubes in the front rack on the bench. Pre-label each tube with the
project ID and tube number (1–8) using a chemical-resistant marker. Place the secondary
containment tray under the sample tubes.

### Step 4 — Verify bench setup

Confirm visible setup before proceeding. The bench must show:

- Two or more **labeled** sample tubes.
- A **secondary containment tray** under the rack.
- A **labeled waste container** on the bench.
- The **BP-1000 pipette display** powered on.
- The reagent bottle's label is **legible** (lot, name, expiry).

If any required item is not visible or not satisfactory, **pause and correct** before
proceeding.

### Step 5 — Aliquot

Aliquot 500 µL of Reagent A into each labeled tube using the BP-1000. Cap each tube
immediately after dispensing.

### Step 6 — Time the equilibration

Allow the aliquots to equilibrate at room temperature for **5 minutes** before proceeding.

### Step 7 — Record observations

Record any visible cloudiness, particles, or color change in the run notes. Such observations
are deviations and must be logged.

### Step 8 — Cleanup and handover

Cap the reagent bottle, dispose of the pipette tip in the labeled waste container, wipe
the bench, and generate the OpenBench handover report.

## 8. Stop conditions

- Required PPE missing.
- Reagent label unreadable.
- Visible spill, leak, or broken glass.
- Any conflict between the SOP, SDS, and equipment manual.
- Unfamiliar reagent in the staging area.

## 9. Deviations

All deviations must be logged in OpenBench with title, severity, and short description.
Deviations of severity "high" or "critical" trigger supervisor review.
